Candle Making in Calgary

Blend your own scents and pour a candle you'll actually burn at home.

All Skill Levels Great for Solo Groups Welcome

Candle making workshops in Calgary are the kind of thing that sounds niche but ends up being one of the most enjoyable evenings you've had in a while. You pick your fragrance combinations, pour the wax, set the wick, and watch it come together. It's creative without being intimidating, and you leave with something that genuinely smells great.

What It's Like

What to Expect

🕯️

Scent blending is the fun part

You'll test and combine fragrance oils to create your own unique scent. It's harder than it sounds and more satisfying when you get it right.

🌡️

Pouring and setting

The host walks you through wax temperature, pour technique, and how to set the wick so it burns evenly.

🏷️

Custom labels and packaging

Most workshops let you name your candle and add a label. Makes it great for gifting.

🧘

Relaxed, low-pressure session

There's no wrong way to make a candle. The whole session has a chill, creative energy to it.

Insider Advice

Tips for Your First Candle Making Event

  • 1 Let the wax cool fully before moving your candle. Rushing it can cause sinkholes in the surface.
  • 2 Stick to two or three fragrance notes when blending. More than that and the scents start competing.
  • 3 Calgary's dry winters mean candles are popular gifts here. Making a batch to give as presents is a great use of the workshop.
  • 4 Bring a friend if you can. Comparing scent combinations you've blended is genuinely entertaining.
  • 5 Mixler lists candle making events in Calgary when they're scheduled. Join the waitlist so you hear about it first.
Common Questions

FAQ

Do I need any experience to make candles? +
No experience needed. The host walks you through every step from scent blending to the final pour.
Do I get to take my candle home the same day? +
Yes. Your candle sets during the session and you take it home at the end of the night.
Can I pick my own scent? +
That's the best part. You'll test and blend fragrance oils to create something that's uniquely yours.
Is this a good activity for a group? +
Very. Comparing your scent choices and watching everyone's candles turn out differently makes for a great group experience.
