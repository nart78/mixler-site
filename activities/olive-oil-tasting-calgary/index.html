Olive Oil Tasting in Calgary

Taste the difference between great olive oil and everything else.

All Skill Levels Great for Solo No Experience Needed

Olive oil tasting events in Calgary are a genuinely eye-opening experience for anyone who cooks. Most people have never tasted truly fresh, high-quality extra virgin olive oil, and the difference from what you buy at a grocery store is remarkable. A guide walks you through the tasting method, helps you identify flavour profiles, and explains what to look for when buying.

What It's Like

What to Expect

🫒

A curated selection of oils

You'll taste 4 to 6 extra virgin olive oils from different regions and harvest times. The range in flavour is genuinely surprising.

👃

Proper tasting technique

The guide teaches you how to warm the oil in your hands, nose it, and taste it correctly to assess quality.

📚

Understanding quality markers

You'll learn what pungency, bitterness, and fruitiness mean in the context of olive oil and why they signal freshness.

🍞

Bread and palate cleansers

Neutral bread and water keep your palate fresh between tastings so each oil gets a fair assessment.

Insider Advice

Tips for Your First Olive Oil Tasting Event

  • 1 Good olive oil should have a peppery finish in the throat. That sensation means the polyphenols are still active.
  • 2 Check the harvest date, not just the best-before date. Fresher oil has more flavour and health benefits.
  • 3 Calgary's specialty food shops increasingly stock high-quality imported olive oils. Knowing what you're tasting helps you choose better.
  • 4 Olive oil tasting pairs beautifully with a cheese board session. The two experiences complement each other well.

Common Questions

FAQ

Do I need food knowledge to enjoy this? +
Not at all. The guide explains everything as you go. You just need to be curious and willing to taste.
How is olive oil tasting different from wine tasting? +
The evaluation process is similar but olive oil is assessed for fruitiness, bitterness, and pungency. You drink from small cups, not glasses.
How many oils will I taste? +
Most events feature 4 to 6 oils, enough to show meaningful differences without overwhelming your palate.
Can I buy the oils after the tasting? +
Many events offer the featured oils for purchase. Check with the organizer for specifics.
